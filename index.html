Biography

I was exposed to coding in January 2016 and was intrigued by it immediately. At the time, I was a HS Foundational Level Math teacher teaching Algebra 1 and Geometry. Although I was satisfied with my career choice, I definitely wondered if there was another field that suited me better. At work, I noticed I enjoyed solving all of the new and crazy Common Core math problems more than I enjoyed teaching the students. Also during Professional Developements, I tended to be the guy who solved all of the example problems that were given to the teachers to experience learning in this era of "new math". I ended up spending hours upon hours of my own freetime solving these complex (or at least deceptively complex) problems. At this point, I had realised I had discovered a new passion and by the middle of the school year, I was contemplating a change.


And then a miracle happened. I was laid off due to a drop in student enrollment. I took it as a sign and threw myself full time into coding in October 2016. I started doing the 'Free Code Camp' cirriculum online and completed all of the projects by the end of February (that's 5 months!). It was definitely a struggle but with the help of a few classes from 'Girl Develope It', countless 'Learn Teach Code' MeetUps, informative YouTube tutorials, weekly workshops through 'CodiePie', and of course thousands of Google searches, I was able to make it through and gain a working knowledge of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript in a relatively short amount of time. My next goal is to learn backend languages and go full stack in my knowledge.


My coding journey has definitely been a challenging one. But one that has kept me fully engaged and satisfied. I love this new path and look forward to learning more and more in the time to come!

Passions

DANCE- Probably my first love. I've danced ever since I was a child. I performed filipino folk dances growning up and that taught me about hard work and discipline. When I got to college, I started studying ballet, modern, and jazz through the California State University Long Beach Dance Department. There, I got the opportunity to preform in several pieces for the school's dance concerts as well choreograph my own work.

THEATRE- I started theatre when I was in High School. It was the perfect way to break out of my near paralysing shyness. I continued to dabble in college and even went to NYC and studied stage acting for a year. Since then, I've performed in community theatre and have written two plays that I have produced and directed through the Hollywood Fringe Festival.

YOGA- I spent about 6 years of my life dedicated to practicing and teaching Ashtanga yoga. The entire time I spent in flip flops and sweat pants. I also took 3 trips to India where I studied under Sri K. Pattabhi Jois. It was a magical time in my life and I'll always cherish those memories.

MATH- The subject of Math always came naturally to me. However, it only became exciting when, as a Math teacher, I was exposed to the new Common Core ways of teaching Math. Suddenly, I was drawing pictures to represent Math concepts and spending hours trying to figure out open ended problems that required me to think outside of the box. And once I figured something out, I had to be able to explain my findings to others. It's definitely a new and exciting approach to Math.
